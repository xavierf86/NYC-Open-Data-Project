Why This Project Matters

Central Park is home to thousands of squirrels. By studying their behaviors and habitats, we can better understand how wildlife adapts to living in a large city like New York.

Did You Know?

The Central Park Squirrel Census was conducted by volunteers who recorded squirrel colors, behaviors, and locations throughout the park.

Project Goal

The goal of this project is to help users explore and analyze data from the Central Park Squirrel Census through interactive tools, filters, and graphs. Users can search for squirrel sightings based on characteristics such as fur color, age, location, and behavior. By examining patterns within the data, visitors can gain a better understanding of the squirrel population living in Central Park and learn how wildlife adapts to an urban environment. This project demonstrates how NYC Open Data can be used to transform large datasets into meaningful and easy-to-understand information.
