Welcome!

Welcome to the Central Park Squirrel Dataset. This website uses data from the NYC Open Data Squirrel Census to help users learn about the squirrels that live throughout Central Park. Explore squirrel colors, behaviors, and locations using real data collected by researchers.

Display


Primary Fur Color

The Primary Fur Color graph examines the distribution of squirrel fur colors recorded in the Central Park Squirrel Census. Most squirrels in Central Park are grey, while smaller populations have black or cinnamon colored fur. By analyzing this data, users can better understand the diversity of the squirrel population and identify which fur colors are most common in the park. Looking at the different fur colors also helps show the variety of squirrels that live in Central Park.

Squirrels Age

The Age graph compares the number of adult and juvenile squirrels recorded during the census. This information helps users understand the age distribution of the squirrel population and provides insight into population growth. By comparing the two groups, users can determine whether most squirrels observed were adults or juveniles. A larger number of younger squirrels may suggest a growing population, while a higher number of adults may indicate a more established population. The graph allows users to quickly compare the age groups and better understand the makeup of the squirrel community in Central Park.

Location

The Location graph compares squirrel sightings that were recorded either above ground or on the ground plane. This allows users to see where squirrels were most commonly observed during the census. Squirrels spend time both in trees and on the ground depending on what they are doing. For example, squirrels may climb trees for safety or rest, while they often come down to the ground to search for food. By comparing the number of sightings in each location, users can better understand where squirrels spend most of their time. The graph provides insight into how squirrels interact with their environment in Central Park.

Squirrels Activities

The Activities graph explores behaviors observed during squirrel sightings, including activities such as running, climbing, eating, and foraging. These observations provide insight into how squirrels spend their time and interact with their environment. By comparing the frequency of different activities, users can determine which behaviors occur most often. The graph also helps show how squirrels adapt to their surroundings and use the resources available to them. This graph helps show what squirrels spend most of their time doing throughout the day.

AM vs PM Shift

The AM vs PM Shift graph compares squirrel observations collected during morning and afternoon observation periods. This allows users to examine whether squirrels were observed more frequently during one part of the day than the other. Differences between the two shifts may suggest changes in activity levels throughout the day. For example, squirrels may be more active during certain hours when searching for food or moving between locations. By comparing AM and PM observations, users can identify patterns in squirrel behavior and gain a better understanding of how activity changes over time. These trends help provide additional insight into the daily routines of Central Park squirrels.
